Liz Garland Deardorff

Coastal CaNEWS Fall 2023

Do you hear that cackling giggle?

My vision of the great beyond includes a fabulous river and I know Bob is having a big time! Coastal’s has lost a paddler that many, myself included, call a mentor, a hero, and inspiration and a friend.  I’m comforted knowing he’s sharing an eddy or a surf wave with paddling friends that went before him. (Hummm, as the new guy on that magical river he may be leaning into the entire wave and just sharing a joyful grin.)

Going on a paddling trip with Bob would guarantee a story and a memory. My gateway to enjoying a river ‘Bob style’ was the result of meeting Larry Gross in a Mary Washington College cartography class. Our early morning weekday trips on the Rappahannock led to weekend forays to chase water, many schemed to fill gaps on the paper map that resided in Bob’s hallway.

I got to help Bob cover the map-- the entirety of Colliers Creek (barbed wire fences and all) to Buffalo Creek into the Maury fed by intense rain that turned into THE floods of 1985. And Bob helped me map new rivers-- leading Bill Hay and I down the upper Gauley for the 1st time, trying to recall where Sweet’s Falls was and finally hearing the happiest scream possible, “this is IT” as he plunged over the lip too late for an eddy! That’s the ‘Bob style’ I loved to follow.
